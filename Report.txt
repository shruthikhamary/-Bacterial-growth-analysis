Title

Computational Analysis of Bacterial Growth Under Antibiotic Stress

Abstract

This project analyzes the growth pattern of E. coli under the presence and absence of ampicillin using computational modeling. Linear regression and exponential models were applied to understand bacterial dynamics.

Introduction

Bacterial growth typically follows exponential or logistic patterns. Antibiotics such as ampicillin inhibit bacterial growth. This project simulates and analyzes these effects using Python.

Methods
Simulated OD600 data
Visualization using matplotlib
Linear regression using scikit-learn
Log transformation for exponential modeling
Results
Growth increased significantly without antibiotic
Growth decreased with antibiotic
Exponential models better matched biological trends
Discussion

Linear regression provides a basic approximation, but exponential growth and decay models better represent bacterial behavior under different conditions.

Conclusion

Computational modeling helps simulate and understand biological processes such as bacterial growth and antibiotic response.

Limitations
Dataset is simulated
Real biological data may show more variation
